## Our most active codebases!

If you're completely new to all of this, it's good to keep in mind that our work can be broadly grouped between **standards** (which describe how things ought to work) and **implementations** (which is the thing that's supposed to be working). ### Rafiki

[Rafiki](https://github.com/interledger/rafiki) is our implementation of Interledger, meant for Account Servicing Entities (ASEs) to **enable Interledger functionality on their users' accounts**. Think of Rafiki like the router in your home or office which allows your device to connect to the internet. Rafiki allows the ASE to connect their system to the Interledger network.

[Sabine Schaller](https://interledgerfoundation.slack.com/team/U03240NN7EZ) leads the Rafiki team. She's the person writing up the monthly [Rafiki updates](https://community.interledger.org/t/rafikiupdates) on our community forem (yes, that's not a spelling error). [Nathan Lie](https://interledgerfoundation.slack.com/team/U05HHTMN9UZ) AKA g'Nathan can answer all your questions about GNAP. If you're asking, what in the world is GNAP, that's a good reason to ping him on Slack.

[Max Kurapov](https://interledgerfoundation.slack.com/team/U044PTD55B7), [Blair Currey](https://interledgerfoundation.slack.com/team/U054WDVT7FZ), [Jason Brewer](https://interledgerfoundation.slack.com/team/U05H66H9TQ8), [Oana Lolea](https://interledgerfoundation.slack.com/team/U07LJEW3NFM) and [Sarah Jones](https://interledgerfoundation.slack.com/team/U01JXS9GPEX) are also folks you can ask for questions around Rafiki. ### Open Payments

[Open Payments](https://github.com/interledger/open-payments) is an open API standard that is used in Rafiki and can be implemented by ASEs to **allow delegated access into user accounts**. It provides a level of unprecendented granularity for third parties who require access to specific permissions into user accounts without exposing data beyond what is granted.

### Interledger Pay

[Interledger Pay](https://github.com/interledger/interledger-pay) is a **simplified payments platform**, where you can easily send or request money from anyone anywhere in the world, if you own a Wallet Address. You can use it to send test money with Interledger test wallet, or real money with a Fynbos account, if you have one. The key people who built and maintain this project are [Timea](https://interledgerfoundation.slack.com/team/U048Z4VU7MY) and [Radu](https://interledgerfoundation.slack.com/team/U048W6E3MHB).

### Testnet

[Testnet](https://github.com/interledger/testnet) is short for Interledger Test Network, which is our open Interledger network that works with test money. It is meant **for ASEs to test their Interledger integration** without the burden of dealing with the reality of various compliance and regulatory issues in their respective jurisdictions.

Anybody who wants to integrate with the Interledger network can automatically peer with the Interledger test wallet and join the testnet. Our team maintains 2 of the assets on testnet, Interledger test wallet and Interledger test boutique.

[Timea Nagy](https://interledgerfoundation.slack.com/team/U048Z4VU7MY) leads the Testnet team, which consists of a group of devs sometimes affectionately referred to as "The Breakpoint Boyz" (like a respectable boyband). Which is a good segway to talk about BreakPoint IT. Although on paper, they are considered contractors, in reality, everyone on the BreakPoint team is an integral part of the ILF tech team, our peoples through and through.

Our respectable boyband consists of [Radu Popa](https://interledgerfoundation.slack.com/team/U048W6E3MHB) (father to the beautiful emoji-worthy cat, Misty), [Beniamin Munteanu](https://interledgerfoundation.slack.com/team/U04H66HHLGM) (AKA Beni), [Dragos Palade](https://interledgerfoundation.slack.com/team/U04DD23QVS9), [Arpad Lengyel (AKA Arpi)](https://interledgerfoundation.slack.com/team/U06S92YRF1S), [Bogdan Sandu](https://interledgerfoundation.slack.com/team/U070D1GQ6Q1) and [Adrian Boros (AKA Adi)](https://interledgerfoundation.slack.com/team/U07KUFTLQJW).

### Web Monetization extension

[Web Monetization](https://github.com/WICG/webmonetization) is one of the Interledger use-cases we are trying to develop in the real world. It is a standard that we are trying to get browsers to implement natively, but that process will take a long time. So for the time being, we are developing our [browser extension](https://github.com/interledger/web-monetization-extension), which will replicate future native browser behaviour, as well as provide an interface for folks to use web monetization to pay websites.

Radu (yes, the same Radu who is a member of The Breakpoint Boyz, he's a busy man!) is leading the engineering effort for the extension, and together with [Sid Vishnoi](https://interledgerfoundation.slack.com/team/U0778QD9FJ5), they are trying to bring the extension to a state where the general public can send money to each others websites.

The [Web Monetization Catchup](https://meet.google.com/fjy-vjef-ogj) (see [meeting notes](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1-xHBeZqPMhys1BPe8qH95yxrcWqGY2zbILUOPLU1OoA/edit)) happens every other Thursday at 3pm GMT+2 (please [convert to your own timezone](https://www.timeanddate.com/worldclock/meeting.html) accordingly). Feel free to ask in the team channel to be added to the meeting.

### Web Monetization tools

[Web Monetization tools](https://github.com/interledger/web-monetization-tools) is meant to support and promote the adoption of Web Monetization by providing tools that let folks generate and add banners, widgets, buttons or exclusive content to their own web-monetized sites. The widgets will also make use of Interledger Pay. If this piques your interest, reach out to [Arpi](https://interledgerfoundation.slack.com/team/U06S92YRF1S) to find out more.

### Project Make Web Monetization Kinda Real

As mentioned above, [Web Monetization](https://github.com/WICG/webmonetization) is a browser standard that we are developing. That's why the repository sits with the WICG instead of Interledger. If you are unfamiliar with [web standards](https://www.w3.org/standards/), they are technical specifications designed to maximize consensus, ensure quality, earn endorsement and adoption by W3C Members and the broader community.

The process of getting from standard to real-world implementation in browsers often takes a long time, so Interledger is developing a web monetization extension that acts as a polyfill for the expected functionalities until the standard gets implemented in browsers, like [Chromium](https://issues.chromium.org/issues/40110471). We are working with [Alexander Surkov](https://interledgerfoundation.slack.com/team/U053Z05B6AE) from [Igalia](https://www.igalia.com/) on the browser implementation side of things.

Otherwise, for extension-related questions, reach out to [Radu](https://interledgerfoundation.slack.com/team/U048W6E3MHB) or [Sid](https://interledgerfoundation.slack.com/team/U0778QD9FJ5).

### Project Telemetry

We want to measure the growth of the Interledger Network to make sure we're on the right track to retiring well (see above). And we have to track the network in order to measure it. Information like how many packets are moving across the network and how much money is getting transfered, things like that. We measure both the Test network and our soon-to-be Live network (as soon as Fynbos and Gatehub fix their compliance problems), using [Grafana](https://grafana.com/) as our observability platform.

[Beni](https://interledgerfoundation.slack.com/team/U04H66HHLGM), [Blair](https://interledgerfoundation.slack.com/team/U054WDVT7FZ) and [Sarah](https://interledgerfoundation.slack.com/team/U01JXS9GPEX) are the reason why we have dashboards with nice charts, so direct your telemetry-related questions to them.

### Fynbos (pronounced "Fain (like rain)"-"Boss (like boss man)")

[Fynbos](https://fynbos.app/) is a digital wallet that supports ILP natively (as succintly put by founder, Adrian Hope-Bailie). Fynbos had spun out from Coil to go through the ardous process of licensing and becoming a full-fledged digital wallet (for more on Coil, please see the history section below). Fynbos has implemented Interledger and once we get past the compliacne hurdle, they should be one of the first nodes on the Interledger Network. If you want to have a chat with them, you can find the Fynbos folks in the [#fynbos-ilf](https://interledgerfoundation.slack.com/archives/C02BLL70QMN) channel.

### Gatehub
